Frequently asked questions

Bridges can be categorized into several types: beam bridges, arch bridges, suspension bridges, cable-stayed bridges, truss bridges, and cantilever bridges, among others.

The lifespan of a bridge varies depending on factors like design, materials used, maintenance, and environmental conditions, but many bridges are designed to last 50 to 100 years or more.

Bridge inspections are conducted regularly to assess structural integrity, identify maintenance needs, and ensure safety for users.

Bridge construction can have environmental effects such as habitat disruption, alteration of water flows, and changes to local ecosystems. However, modern designs often incorporate measures to mitigate these impacts.

In earthquake-prone areas, engineers design bridges to withstand seismic forces using techniques such as base isolation (isolating the structure from the ground motion), flexible bearings, and reinforced concrete or steel components that can absorb energy.
